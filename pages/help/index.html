MintPool Help

Frequently Asked Questions

What is profit-switching?

A profit-switching pool is a virtual network of mining rigs that automatically mine the most profitable coin.

How am I paid for mining?

When the pool finds a block in any of the altcoins it mines for, the reward is split proportionally amongst it's contributors (less a pool fee, of course). However, depending on which pool a worker mines for, the pool pays out in a converted Dogecoin or Mintcoin. Payouts occur daily, usually between 2300 - 0300 CST, depending on trading volume. This convenience allows miners to focus more on mining, and less on using the markets to convert to their desired coin.

How do I know which coin I am paid in?

MintPool has two stratums, or servers that accept pool connections over a the internet. The stratum host for MintPool is stratum+tcp://mintpool.us. However, depending on which port you connect to, you will be paid out in that port's assigned coin. For Dogecoin, use port 3333, for Mintcoin use port 4444 or 3334. Please do not forget: you must provide a username to the pool of the receiving address for the coins you earn. Failure to do so will treat any earned coins as a donation to the pool!

What is profitability?

Profitability is the ratio of how much you can potentially earn by mining the specified coin rather than mining Bitcoin. e.g., a profitability of 1.2 means that the coin is currently 20% more profitable to mine than Bitcoin. This assumes that you immediately take the coin and trade it in for Bitcoins.

What coins does this pool mine?

MintPool mines Scrypt-based altcoins. Scrypt is the name of the algorithm used by some coins as a proof-of-work. By design, the output (aka hash) of this algorithm is hard to predict, and the lucky miner who guesses it, earns a reward. It is the goal of this pool to successfully guess the hashes of the most profitable coins. Here are the coins MintPool currently mines:

  • Dogecoin (DOGE)
  • EarthCoin (EAC)
  • LiteCoin (LTC)
  • CasinoCoin (CSC)
  • Lottocoin (LOTTO)
  • KittehCoin (MEOW)
  • Catcoin (CAT)

What is a proof-of-work?

Simply put, proof-of-work is a puzzle that miners are rewarded for solving. They are typically difficult to solve and the payouts for solving them are unique to each coin.

What are pool fees?

Pool fees are the price miners pay to be part of a pool. The fees pay for server upkeep and employee payroll. The pool fee for MintPool is a low 1.5%

What is Dogecoin?

A crypto-currency based off of an internet meme of a Shiba Inu. You can learn more about it on the Dogecoin subreddit.

What is Mintcoin?

MintCoin is an innovative, secure and energy efficient coin. It uses a faster Proof-Of-Work distribution mechanism to distribute the initial coins, then after 5 weeks the coin is basically transferred to a pure Proof-Of-Stake coin, where the generation of the coin is mainly through the Proof-Of-Stake interests.
